The program starts by initializing the stress score to zero.
A set of nine stress-related questions is presented to the user one by one.
Each question focuses on factors affecting student mental stress, such as education level, academic workload, exam frequency, family support, bullying, job opportunities, financial condition, and personal relationships.
The user selects one answer from the available options for every question.
Each selected answer is assigned a predefined score based on its stress impact.
Lower stress-related answers receive lower scores, while higher stress-related answers receive higher scores.
The score of each answer is added to the total stress score.
This process continues until all nine questions are completed.
After collecting all responses, the total stress score is calculated.
The final score is analyzed to determine the stress level of the student.
If the score is low, the result is classified as Low Stress.
If the score is in the middle range, the result is classified as Moderate Stress.
If the score is high, the result is classified as High Stress.
Finally, the total stress score and stress level are displayed as output.
